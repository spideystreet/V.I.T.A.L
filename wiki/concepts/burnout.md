# Burnout

## WHO ICD-11 Definition

Burn-out is classified under **"Factors influencing health status or contact with health services"**, not as a medical disease.

**Definition:** "Syndrome conceptualized as resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed."

### Three Dimensions (ICD-11)
1. **Feelings of energy depletion or exhaustion**
2. **Increased mental distance from one's job**, or feelings of negativism/cynicism related to one's job
3. **Reduced professional efficacy**

**Important:** Burn-out "refers specifically to phenomena in the occupational context and should not be applied to describe experiences in other areas of life."

## Epidemiology (France)

### Pre-Covid vs Post-Covid
- **2019 (Santé publique France):**
  - Work-related psychological suffering: 5.9% women, 2.7% men (double 2007 figures)
  - Burn-out specifically: 0.7% women, 0.34% men

- **2022 (OpinionWay x Empreinte Humaine):**
  - **34% of employees report being in burn-out**
  - 13% in severe burn-out
  - **~2.5 million people affected**

### Estimates Vary
- **300,000–500,000** (strict definition)
- **480,000 workers** in psychological suffering at work (7% in burn-out)
- **2.5 million** (broad definition)

**Key insight:** Even occupational health bodies struggle to have clear real-time data → justifies continuous monitoring via V.I.T.A.L

## Economic Impact

- **INRS study (2007):** Cost of work-related stress in France = **€2–3 billion per year**, mainly due to psychiatric disorders

## Risk Factors (French Gov)

- Workload and time pressure
- Lack of autonomy and decision-making power
- Poor recognition and unclear expectations
- Quality of management and social support
- Work–life balance issues

**Legal:** Employers have **legal obligation to prevent psychosocial risks** under French labour law

## Mental Health Trends (2023)

- Mental health indicators in France **continued to deteriorate in 2023**
- **18–24-year-olds** show highest levels of depressive symptoms and psychological distress
- **V.I.T.A.L implication:** Young professionals in high-pressure jobs = priority target

## V.I.T.A.L Positioning

1. **Early-detection and prevention tool** for ICD-11-recognized phenomenon
2. **Not a diagnostic or treatment tool**
3. Can safely rely on ICD-11 three-dimension framework for:
   - Symptom questions during [[weekly-vocal-checkup]]
   - Risk scoring combining subjective answers + biometric data ([[hrv]], [[sleep]])

## Occupational Health Integration

**INRS explicitly states:**
> "Le service de prévention et de santé au travail peut également aider l'entreprise à repérer les facteurs de risques professionnels en lien avec les cas de burnout portés à sa connaissance."

**V.I.T.A.L role:**
- First-line signal and support
- Help identify early warning signs at individual level
- Aggregated anonymized metrics for occupational health services
