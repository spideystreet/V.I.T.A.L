# HRV – Heart Rate Variability

## What HRV Measures

**Definition:** Variation in time between consecutive heartbeats.

**Physiology:**
- Reflects **balance between sympathetic (stress) and parasympathetic (recovery) nervous systems**
- High HRV = better recovery, adaptability, resilience
- Low HRV = fatigue, stress, overtraining

## Scientific Validation

### Stress Marker (Kim & Cheon Meta-Analysis)
- **Psychological stress reliably associated with decreased parasympathetic HRV**
- Key metrics: RMSSD and HF power
- Stress accompanied by:
  - Higher heart rate
  - Altered LF/HF ratio (autonomic imbalance)

### Mental Disorders (Umbrella Review)
- **Reduced HRV consistently associated with:**
  - Depression
  - Anxiety
  - PTSD
- HRV = marker of **emotion regulation capacity and resilience**, not only acute stress

### Workplace Studies
- **Nurses study:** HRV differences by shift type, workload, unit characteristics
- **BMJ Open physicians study:** First UK study planning continuous HRV monitoring to relate physiological stress to burnout
- **Conclusion:** Continuous HRV-based stress monitoring in real workplace conditions is **feasible**

## Wearable Measurement

### Accuracy & Best Practices
- PPG vs ECG differences
- Artefact correction and data quality issues
- Best practices:
  - Minimal recording duration
  - Posture control
  - Time of day consistency
- **Key principle:** Patterns and trends over single measurements

### Apple Watch / HealthKit
- HRV available via HealthKit
- **Question:** Real-time or batch (overnight) only?

## V.I.T.A.L Application

### Core Use
1. **Track low HRV episodes** as potential stress spikes
2. **Use trends** (sustained HRV reduction + [[sleep]]) as early markers of burnout risk ([[burnout]])
3. Combine HRV level + variability over weeks with self-reported exhaustion → estimate **vulnerability to burnout**

### User Explanation
> "We don't care about one HRV number, but how your body recovers over time."

### Limitations to Communicate
- Device-specific limitations ([[apple]])
- Need for consistent measurement conditions
- Individual baselines vary
