# Sleep – Duration & Burnout Link

## Key Finding (Healthcare Professionals Study)

**Strong association between short sleep duration and burnout.**

### Effect Sizes
- Compared to ≥7h sleep/night:
  - **<7h linked to 17.18x higher odds of burnout in women**
  - **<7h linked to 8.33x higher odds of burnout in men** (after adjusting for confounders)
- Demonstrates **dose–response pattern** between sleep deprivation and burnout risk

## V.I.T.A.L Application

### Core Input
- **Weekly average sleep = central input in V.I.T.A.L's risk score**

### Actionable Rules
1. **Warn users** when average sleep <7h AND [[hrv]] is low
2. **Frame with evidence:** "Lack of sleep is one of the strongest quantitative predictors of [[burnout]]."
