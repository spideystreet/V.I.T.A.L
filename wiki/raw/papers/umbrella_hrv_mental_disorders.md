## Transdiagnostic Role of HRV

- Umbrella review and meta-analysis of HRV across multiple mental disorders.[web:128][web:129]
- Overall pattern: **reduced HRV is consistently associated with many mental health conditions** (depression, anxiety, PTSD, etc.).[web:128][web:129]
- HRV is considered a marker of **emotion regulation capacity and resilience**, not only acute stress.

## Relevance for V.I.T.A.L

- Supports using HRV as a **global resilience / recovery signal** for mental health, not just momentary stress.
- V.I.T.A.L can:
  - Combine HRV level + variability over weeks with self-reported exhaustion to estimate **vulnerability to burnout**.
  - Explain to users that HRV is linked to both **stress and emotional regulation**.
