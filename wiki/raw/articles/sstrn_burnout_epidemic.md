## Difficulty Quantifying Burn-out

- Burn-out is **not officially recognised as an occupational disease** in France, making it hard to quantify precisely.[web:113]
- SSTRN reports:
  - Between **300,000 and 500,000 people** in burn-out across studies.[web:113]
  - Empreinte Humaine barometer: **2.5 million people** in burn-out when using a broader definition.[web:113][web:157][web:160]
  - Institut de veille sanitaire: **480,000 workers** in psychological suffering at work, **7% of them in burn-out**.[web:113]

## Relevance for V.I.T.A.L

- Confirms wide ranges in estimates, but all point to a **major and growing issue**.
- Useful to show that **even occupational health bodies struggle to have clear real-time data**, which justifies:
  - Continuous individual monitoring via V.I.T.A.L.
  - Aggregated anonymised indicators to support occupational health services.

## Unknown / To Confirm

- Whether burn-out will eventually be included on the list of recognised occupational diseases in France.
