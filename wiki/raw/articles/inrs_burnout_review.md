## Definition and Mechanism

- Burn-out is presented as a **set of reactions to chronic professional stress** in situations where **invested commitment is very high**.[web:112]
- It mainly affects **people highly engaged in their work**, with a strong sense of responsibility and often high emotional involvement.[web:112]
- INRS emphasises that prevention must **act on work organization and psychosocial risk factors**, not just on individual resilience.[web:112]

## Role of Occupational Health Services

- INRS explicitly states:  
  > “Le service de prévention et de santé au travail peut également aider l’entreprise à repérer les facteurs de risques professionnels en lien avec les cas de burnout portés à sa connaissance.”[web:112]
- In other words, **occupational health and prevention services should help detect and analyse risk factors at company level** when burn-out cases appear.

## Relevance for V.I.T.A.L

- This sentence is exactly where **V.I.T.A.L can plug in as a first-line signal and support**:
  - V.I.T.A.L can help **identify early warning signs at individual level** (HRV, sleep, self-reported exhaustion) before severe burn-out.
  - Aggregated, anonymized metrics could help **occupational health services detect teams with elevated risk** and suggest organisational interventions.
- V.I.T.A.L can be positioned as:
  - A **pre-screening and early-warning tool** before calling a doctor or occupational physician.
  - A way to **document work-related risk factors** through weekly check-ups and trends, which occupational health services can then analyse.
