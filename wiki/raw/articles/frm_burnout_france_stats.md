## Epidemiology (pre- and post-Covid)

- Santé publique France analysis (2019):  
  - Work-related psychological suffering: **5.9% of women** and **2.7% of men**, double 2007 figures.[web:122]  
  - Burn-out specifically: **0.7% of women** and **0.34% of men**.[web:122]
- OpinionWay x Empreinte Humaine barometer (2022):  
  - **34% of employees** report being in burn-out, including **13% in severe burn-out**.[web:122][web:157]  
  - This corresponds to **about 2.5 million people**.[web:122][web:158][web:161]
- FRM notes that estimates vary but converging studies show a **major increase** in work-related distress and burn-out since Covid.[web:122]

## Economic Cost

- INRS study (2007 data, published 2010) estimated the **cost of work-related stress in France at €2–3 billion per year**, mainly due to psychiatric disorders.[web:122]
