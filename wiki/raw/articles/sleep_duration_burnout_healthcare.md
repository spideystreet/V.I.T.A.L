# Sleep Duration and Burnout in Healthcare (raw notes)

**Primary source**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10806404/  
**Reading date**: 2026-04-07  
**Status**: raw (do not modify)

---

## Main Results

- Study on healthcare professionals: strong association between **short sleep duration and burnout**.[web:132][web:135]
- Compared to ≥7h sleep/night, sleeping <7h was linked to:
  - Up to **17.18x higher odds of burnout in women**.
  - Around **8.33x higher odds in men**, after adjusting for confounders.[web:132]
- Demonstrates a **dose–response pattern** between sleep deprivation and burnout risk.

## Relevance for V.I.T.A.L

- Justifies making **weekly average sleep** a central input in V.I.T.A.L’s risk score.
- V.I.T.A.L can:
  - Warn users when their average sleep is <7h and HRV is low.
  - Frame this with: “lack of sleep is one of the strongest quantitative predictors of burnout.”
