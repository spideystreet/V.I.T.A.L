## National Positioning

- The French government explicitly recognises **mental health at work as a major public health issue**.[web:120]
- Communication is part of the “Parlons santé mentale !” initiative, aiming to reduce stigma and promote early action.[web:120]

## Risk Factors Listed

- Organisational and psychosocial factors include:  
  - Workload and time pressure.  
  - Lack of autonomy and decision-making power.  
  - Poor recognition and unclear expectations.  
  - Quality of management and social support.  
  - Work–life balance issues.[web:120]
- Employers have a **legal obligation to prevent psychosocial risks** under French labour law.[web:120]

## Relevance for V.I.T.A.L

- Confirms that burn-out and stress at work are not just “individual problems” but recognised **risks to be prevented**.
- V.I.T.A.L can be framed as:
  - A **tool that helps employees monitor their own risk** (HRV, sleep, exhaustion).
  - A **supporting element of a broader company prevention strategy**, never a replacement for organisational change.
